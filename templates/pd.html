We Are Students

iSchool Initiative‘s professional development is unique in that it is taught by students, for educators. This new perspective offers a fresh, engaging workshop that is less about instruction, and more about an opportunity to have fun while you‘re learning. Our team members want to serve as guides to make you comfortable, curious, and excited. The goal is to show ways in which youth are already utilizing mobile technology to enhance their studies, so that educators can go back to class and meet their students on common ground.

Our PD is Different

  • Personalized

    Learning in its purest form should be focused on the individual inside of a flexible learning environment with multiple content delivery mediums for students and teachers alike.

  • Collaborative

    Two or more learners capitalizing on one another‘s ideas, skills, and resources towards a common goal or problem can greatly enhance the learning process for all parties involved... including us.

  • Digital

    One of the most productive forms of learning is the art of Find, Filter, and Apply. As mountains of new information becomes available at the touch of a button, the importance of this concept continues to grow.

Over the years, iSchool Initiative has worked with thousands of educators to bring you some of the most intuitive Professional Learning available. Technology has changed the game for us and we pride ourselves in sharing our way of thinking.

We Inspire

Becoming a Mobile Learner

Embark upon a journey through the dawn of the information age with college student, Travis Allen. Experience the life of a true digital native who boldly refused to accept the status quo in the classroom. Learn how he uses his tablet, and only his tablet, in all of his courses. More importantly learn how you can be a part of a revolution for change in education.

The New Reality

On the horizon is world that we could never have been imagined even thirty years ago. We are quickly progressing toward a society where human capital, or the creative ingenuity unique to human beings, is becoming our greatest asset. Beginning with words of some of the great future thinkers of our time, Ken then asks, “What implications do these have for education? Where will a teacher fit in?” He goes on to discuss the amazing opportunities and foreboding pitfalls of a digital future, eventually leading to one stark conclusion: technology won‘t save us without teachers.

Mobile in Rural Areas

This keynote details the use of technology in rural education with a focus on tablet devices. We discuss how mobile is used both in and out of the classroom setting for the betterment of scholastic and personal endeavors. The keynote will also look into how schools are using these technologies and will reference evidence for the utility of mobile tech. The widespread use and implementation of these technologies is important for the future of our education system and integral to leveling the playing field.

Awakening Your Creative Genius

Awakening Your Creative Genius does exactly what the title suggests. It causes people to become aware of their own creative potential and understand how to harness it in education. This keynote takes creativity out of the confines of just art and music. Keep an open mind as this experience is not meant to teach the audience anything new, but rather to get people to see what they already know from a different perspective—causing a paradigm shift.
